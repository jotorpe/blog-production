Research

I started my formative career at the University of Granada, obtaining a degree in Civil Engineering with a specialization in Transport and Urban Services. Shortly before finishing the degree I joined the Non Destructive Evaluation laboratory (renamed Ultrasonics Lab TEP-959) of the UGR. When I first joined, back in September 2015, I intended to differentiate myself from the rest of the students thanks to the innovative projects they were carrying out. The application of ultrasound as a tool to respond to the constant uncertainties that occur within materials or structures in operation, that is, entering the world of reliability and mechanical behavior prognosis was imposed on me as something very attractive.

The first steps led me to the project of predicting premature birth, a pathology that affects millions of children a year and puts their lives at risk in the short term. Thanks to this, I have been able to work with both medical and other physical specialists, who have helped us to optimize our technology. I have performed various safety and efficiency tests, improved multiple designs, developed computational algorithms for biomechanical modeling, analyzed sensor data and trained new colleagues in the methodology of our laboratory, something that feels comforting to say the least. In addition, as a researcher one has a very dynamic role, one does not stay stuck in a specific topic, which allows one to be creative, think differently and be open to new knowledge.
